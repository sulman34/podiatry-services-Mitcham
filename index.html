Why Runners Are Prone to Foot Injuries

Running exerts repeated force on the feet, which can lead to various injuries over time. Every step a runner takes involves their foot absorbing a force that’s several times their body weight. This constant stress, combined with improper footwear, overtraining, or running on uneven surfaces, can result in injuries that may sideline even the most experienced runners.

Some common foot injuries that runners face include:

  1. Plantar Fasciitis – Inflammation of the thick band of tissue running across the bottom of the foot.
  2. Stress Fractures – Small cracks in the bones of the feet, often caused by overuse.
  3. Achilles Tendinitis – Inflammation of the Achilles tendon, which connects the calf muscles to the heel.
  4. Blisters and Calluses – Common issues caused by friction between the foot and the shoe.
  5. Runner's Knee and Shin Splints – While these issues occur slightly higher up, they often stem from poor foot mechanics.

Common Foot Injuries in Runners and How to Avoid Them

Below are some of the most common foot injuries faced by runners and tips from Mitcham podiatry professionals on how to avoid them.

1. Plantar Fasciitis

What It Is:
Plantar fasciitis is an inflammation of the plantar fascia, the band of tissue that runs across the bottom of your foot and connects your heel to your toes. It causes stabbing pain in the heel, especially in the morning.

How to Avoid It:

  • Stretching: Perform daily stretches for your calves and the bottom of your feet. A common stretch is the towel stretch, where you pull a towel wrapped around the ball of your foot toward you.
  • Supportive Shoes: Choose running shoes that offer proper arch support and have cushioned soles.
  • Rest and Recovery: Don’t ignore early signs of pain. Rest when you feel discomfort, and gradually increase the intensity of your running program.

2. Stress Fractures

What It Is:
Stress fractures are tiny cracks in the bones, commonly affecting the metatarsals (the long bones in the foot). They usually result from overuse or increasing running mileage too quickly.

How to Avoid It:

  • Increase Mileage Gradually: Follow the 10% rule – don’t increase your weekly mileage by more than 10%.
  • Cross-Training: Incorporate low-impact exercises like swimming or cycling to reduce stress on the feet.
  • Strength Training: Strengthen the muscles around your feet and legs to absorb the impact of running.

3. Achilles Tendinitis

What It Is:
Achilles tendinitis is the inflammation of the Achilles tendon, which connects the calf muscles to the heel. This injury causes pain and stiffness in the lower leg and heel, often making it difficult to run or walk.

How to Avoid It:

  • Strengthen Calves: Strengthening the calf muscles with exercises like heel raises can reduce strain on the Achilles tendon.
  • Stretch Regularly: Stretch your Achilles and calf muscles before and after runs.
  • Avoid Sudden Increases in Activity: Build your running intensity slowly to give your tendons time to adapt.

4. Blisters and Calluses

What It Is:
Blisters and calluses are caused by repeated friction between the foot and the shoe, leading to painful sores or thickened skin on the feet.

How to Avoid It:

  • Wear Proper-Fitting Shoes: Make sure your running shoes fit well and don’t rub against your feet.
  • Moisture-Wicking Socks: Wear socks made from materials that wick away moisture to reduce friction.
  • Lubricants: Applying a small amount of lubricant to problem areas can reduce friction and prevent blisters.

5. Runner's Knee and Shin Splints

What It Is:
Though not directly a foot injury, runner's knee and shin splints often stem from poor foot mechanics or inadequate footwear. Runner's knee causes pain around the kneecap, while shin splints lead to pain in the front of the lower leg.

How to Avoid It:

  • Choose Supportive Shoes: Wear shoes that provide adequate arch support and cushioning for your feet.
  • Gait Analysis: Have a podiatrist evaluate your running form and suggest corrections if necessary.
  • Strengthen Supporting Muscles: Focus on strengthening your hips, glutes, and core to reduce the load on your knees and shins.

Tips to Prevent Foot Injuries from Mitcham Podiatry Experts

  1. Warm-Up and Cool Down
    Properly warming up before a run and cooling down afterward is essential for injury prevention. Dynamic stretches before running and static stretches after can improve flexibility and reduce the risk of injury.
  2. Pay Attention to Pain
    Runners often push through pain, thinking it's part of the process. However, ignoring foot pain can lead to more severe injuries. If you notice persistent pain, consult a podiatrist to identify and address the issue early.
  3. Rotate Your Shoes
    Running in the same pair of shoes every day can increase the risk of injury. Rotate between two or more pairs of running shoes to reduce wear and tear and ensure proper support.
  4. Run on Soft Surfaces
    Whenever possible, run on softer surfaces like grass or dirt trails instead of hard concrete. Softer surfaces help absorb some of the impact and reduce strain on your feet.
  5. Strengthen Your Feet
    Exercises like toe curls, foot stretches, and balance work can strengthen the muscles in your feet, improving stability and reducing injury risk.
